Next Online Meeting

Select an option below to configure the presentation of your Next Meeting section:

The homepage will scan your course’s Calendar for entries that contain a Zoom or Teams meeting link in the description. A Join Meeting button will display shortly before the meeting is scheduled to start.

Instructions for setting this up can be found in the Post Your Online Class Opens in a new tab guide.

Note: If this option is selected and no calendar entries with a Zoom or Teams link are found in your course, the homepage will display “No upcoming online meetings to display at this time.” instead.

The homepage will scan your course’s Calendar for entries that have a specific word of your choice in their titles. For example, if you set the word “Class” as your tag, the system will find entries called “Weekly Class”, or “Week 13 Class”, or “Classroom Time” and always present the next scheduled match within the Next Meeting section.

The tag can be anything you want, but words like “Class”, “Meeting”, “Lecture”, etc. tend to communicate clearly what they represent when students view your calendar.

Note: If this option is selected and no calendar entries with a matching tag are found in your course, the system will display “No upcoming meetings to display at this time.” instead.

Simply type a description of when and how your class meets.

A short, clear description containing only the time and meeting location details tends to work best. E.g.,

  • Thursdays from 10am to 12pm, Doon 1C5
  • Tuesdays from 9am to 11am.
    Fridays from 3pm to pm.
  • This class is asynchronous and does not have a scheduled lecture.

The Next Meeting section will not be presented on your course’s Homepage.

Reminder to Submit Demo Video for Judges

Saturday, April 11, 2026, 11:22 PM

Hi everyone,

Reminder: Presentation for Demo Day Judging submissions are due via Dropbox by April 12, 2026 at 11:59 PM.

Submission Requirements

  • Submit a single MP4 video (team submission)

  • Video must be 10 minutes or less

  • All team members must appear on webcam

  • Slides must be presented in full-screen presentation mode

  • Every team member must contribute to the presentation

What to Include in Your Presentation

Your video should tell a clear product story and include:

  • The problem being solved

  • The purpose of your system

  • Target users

  • Key features that deliver the solution

  • Overall value of the system

You must also include a live demo of your application integrated into the presentation.

Demo Requirements

Your demo should:

  • Be clearly visible and easy to follow

  • Show at least four completed use cases, such as:

    • Viewing dashboards

    • Creating or managing data

    • Retrieving stored information

    • Completing a core workflow

  • Be smooth, well-paced, and directly connected to your product story

Required Slides

Your presentation must include:

  • Title Slide

  • Team Member Photos

  • Problem Statement

  • Business Purpose Statement

  • Project Features

  • Security Practices

  • UX Principles

  • Business Benefits

  • Tech Stack

  • Summary / Conclusion

Technical Expectations

  • The system should appear stable and fully functional

  • Core features must work and be clearly demonstrated

  • The overall experience should feel cohesive and professional

Please make sure you test your recording setup in advance to avoid issues on submission day.

Let me know if you have any questions.
